My Readings

Animal Farm: When Revolution Turns Sour

Ever wondered what would happen if animals ran a farm? George Orwell's Animal Farm is a deceptively simple fable that packs a powerful punch. Tired of being exploited by the cruel farmer Mr. Jones, the animals of Manor Farm stage a revolution, driving him out and establishing their own society based on the principles of Animalism: "All animals are equal." Led by the intelligent pigs, Napoleon and Snowball, the animals work together, dreaming of a utopian future free from human tyranny. However, power corrupts. A bitter rivalry develops between Napoleon and Snowball, ultimately leading to Snowball's expulsion and Napoleon's rise as a totalitarian dictator. The pigs gradually seize control, rewriting history, manipulating the other animals, and exploiting them even more ruthlessly than Mr. Jones ever did. The seven commandments of Animalism are subtly altered to suit the pigs' selfish agenda. Animal Farm is a chilling allegory of the Russian Revolution, exposing the dangers of unchecked power, propaganda, and the corruption of ideals. It's a reminder that revolutions can be betrayed, and that those who promise equality may become the worst oppressors of all. A timely and unsettling read that will leave you questioning the nature of power and the meaning of freedom.

Oliver Twist: A Child's Struggle in a Cruel World

Imagine a world where poverty is a crime, and orphans are treated worse than stray dogs. That's the London Oliver Twist is born into. This classic novel by Charles Dickens follows the harrowing journey of Oliver, a young boy born in a workhouse, as he navigates the brutal underbelly of Victorian society. Oliver escapes the workhouse and falls in with a gang of child thieves led by the manipulative Fagin. He encounters the Artful Dodger, Nancy, Bill Sikes, Mr Brownlow, and a host of other colorful and often dangerous characters. He yearns for a life of kindness and respect, desperately trying to escape the clutches of the criminal world that threatens to consume him. Dickens masterfully portrays the stark contrast between the grinding poverty and callous indifference of the wealthy and the resilience of the human spirit, even in the most desperate circumstances. Oliver Twist isn't just a story about a boy's survival; it's a powerful indictment of social injustice and a celebration of the enduring hope for goodness in a world rife with cruelty. Will Oliver find the family and security he so desperately craves, or will he be forever trapped by his unfortunate beginnings? Read on to find out!
